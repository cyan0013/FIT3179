At a Glance: The Prevalence of Victim Reporting in Victoria

Reporting Locations in Victoria

This map shows the rate of victimisation in Victoria and how this is impacted by region. Visually most areas look to have around a rate 2,000 to 4,000 reports per 100,000 people. The exceptions are Melbourne (North West), Mildura (Western) and Latrobe (Eastern). Melbourne has both the highest rate of victimisation(7.27%) and highest number of reports (13,638), making it the riskiest area in Victoria.

Reporter Demographic

Overall, it seems men and women are equal in their reporting in 2020, however, women are more likely to report crimes against them physically, while men report property and deception offences. There are no other significant offences

Types of Offences Reported

Upon further examination, we found the top 5 offences were: assault, property damage, burglary, theft and sexual offences. Theft has consistenly been the most reported, and has risen since 2019. Assault has also steadily risen past burglary and property offences.
